In the gritty streets of 19th-century Batavia, Pitung—a master of silat trained by the mystic Haji Naipin—becomes a folk hero by robbing corrupt Dutch officials and redistributing wealth to the oppressed Betawi people. As colonial forces close in, his legend grows through daring escapes and public defiance, until a fateful betrayal leads to his martyrdom, cementing his legacy as Jakarta’s iconic rebel whose spirit outlives the bullet that felled him.
